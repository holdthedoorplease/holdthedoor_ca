Immigrating to Canada from the USA#

Canada offers various immigration pathways for U.S. citizens and permanent residents seeking to establish permanent residency. This guide outlines the primary programs and processes available.

Express Entry System#

Overview

The Express Entry system is Canada’s primary method for managing applications for permanent residence from skilled workers. It encompasses three federal economic immigration programs:

  • Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP): For individuals with foreign work experience in skilled occupations.

  • Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP): For qualified tradespersons with job offers or certifications in Canada.

  • Canadian Experience Class (CEC): For individuals with Canadian work experience in skilled occupations.

Application Process

  1. Determine Eligibility: Assess qualifications based on factors such as age, education, work experience, and language proficiency.

  2. Create an Express Entry Profile: Submit a profile to the Express Entry pool, where candidates are ranked using the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS).

  3. Receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA): Candidates with the highest CRS scores receive ITAs during regular draws.

  4. Submit Permanent Residence Application: After receiving an ITA, submit a complete application within 60 days.

Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS)

The CRS awards points based on:

  • Core Human Capital Factors: Age, education, language proficiency, and work experience.

  • Skill Transferability Factors: Combination of education and work experience.

  • Additional Factors: Provincial nomination, job offer, Canadian education, and sibling in Canada.

A provincial nomination adds 600 points to a candidate’s CRS score, effectively guaranteeing an ITA.

Processing Times

Express Entry applications are typically processed within six months.

Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)#

Overview

The PNP allows provinces and territories to nominate individuals for permanent residence based on regional labor market needs. Each province has its own streams targeting specific groups, such as skilled workers, entrepreneurs, or students.

Application Process

  1. Choose a Province/Territory: Research and select a province that aligns with your skills and intentions.

  2. Apply for Nomination: Submit an application to the province’s immigration program.

  3. Receive Provincial Nomination: If nominated, receive a certificate to include in your federal application.

  4. Apply for Permanent Residence: Submit a federal application to IRCC, including the provincial nomination.

Express Entry-Linked Streams

Many provinces have Express Entry-aligned streams, allowing candidates to enter the Express Entry pool and receive an additional 600 CRS points upon nomination.

Family Sponsorship#

Overview

Canadian citizens and permanent residents can sponsor close family members for permanent residence.

Eligible Relationships

  • Spouse or Common-Law Partner

  • Dependent Children

  • Parents and Grandparents: Subject to annual quotas and specific requirements.

Application Process

  1. Submit Sponsorship Application: The sponsor applies to IRCC to sponsor the family member.

  2. Submit Permanent Residence Application: The sponsored person applies for permanent residence.

  3. Processing and Decision: IRCC assesses both applications concurrently.

Financial Requirements

Sponsors must demonstrate the ability to financially support the sponsored person and meet income thresholds.

Start-Up Visa Program#

Overview

This program targets entrepreneurs with innovative business ideas that have the support of designated organizations in Canada.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Qualifying Business: The business must be incorporated in Canada, with essential operations occurring domestically.

  • Letter of Support: Obtain a commitment from a designated organization (venture capital fund, angel investor group, or business incubator).

  • Language Proficiency: Meet minimum language requirements in English or French.

  • Settlement Funds: Demonstrate sufficient funds to settle in Canada.

Application Process

  1. Secure Support: Pitch your business idea to a designated organization and obtain a Letter of Support.

  2. Apply for Permanent Residence: Submit an application to IRCC, including the Letter of Support.

  3. Work Permit (Optional): Apply for a temporary work permit to start building the business while the permanent residence application is processed.

Additional Pathways#

Agri-Food Pilot

Aimed at addressing labor shortages in the agri-food sector, this pilot provides a pathway to permanent residence for experienced, non-seasonal workers in specific industries and occupations.

Atlantic Immigration Program

Designed to attract skilled workers to Canada’s Atlantic provinces (New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, Prince Edward Island, and Newfoundland and Labrador), this employer-driven program offers a pathway to permanent residence.

Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot

This community-driven program aims to spread the benefits of economic immigration to smaller communities by creating a path to permanent residence for skilled foreign workers who want to work and live in participating communities.
